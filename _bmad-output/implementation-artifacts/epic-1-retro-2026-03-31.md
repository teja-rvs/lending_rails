# Epic 1 Retrospective: Secure Admin Access and Operational Workspace

**Project:** `lending_rails`  
**Epic:** `1`  
**Retrospective Style:** Light-touch success retro with one key process improvement  
**Facilitator:** Bob (Scrum Master)  
**Project Lead:** RVS

## Epic Review

### Outcome

Epic 1 delivered the planned secure access foundation for the product. All four stories were completed:

- `1-1` Initialize the Rails operational foundation
- `1-2` Seed the admin account and secure access rules
- `1-3` Admin login with clear feedback
- `1-4` Authenticated workspace entry and logout

The epic established the Rails-native authentication baseline, admin-only access boundary, login flow, protected workspace entry, and logout flow without drifting into later borrower or dashboard scope.

### What Went Well

- Implementation stayed closely aligned with the story sequence and intended scope.
- The team preserved the Rails-native authentication direction instead of introducing unnecessary stack changes.
- Request-level regression coverage improved across the epic, especially around authentication, redirects, and protected-route behavior.
- The epic produced a usable, stable operational foundation for the next phase of product work.

### Challenges

- The login page needed UI course correction after implementation.
- The main correction areas were spacing and the placement of the `Forgot password?` action.
- The issue was not functional breakage, but incomplete UI sign-off against the intended design.

### Key Insight

UI-facing stories should not be considered complete on functional behavior alone. Completion should require both:

- wireframe alignment
- functional completion

## Next Epic Preparation

### Epic 2 Readiness

Epic 2 remains ready to proceed as planned. No major architectural or scope corrections were identified during this retrospective.

### Carry-Forward Improvement

- Apply a wireframe check alongside the functional check before signing off any UI-facing story.

## Action Items

### Process

1. Update the working definition of done for UI-facing stories to require both wireframe alignment and functional completion.  
Owner: Project team  
Success criteria: UI stories are reviewed against the wireframe before being marked complete.

## Risks and Residual Notes

- No major readiness blockers were identified for Epic 2.
- Minor technical cleanup signals remain in the story trail, but none were considered blocking for the next epic.

## Closing Summary

Epic 1 was a successful foundation epic. The main learning was process-oriented rather than structural: UI work needs explicit wireframe validation in addition to functional validation. With that adjustment, the team appears ready to continue into Epic 2 without broader course correction.
