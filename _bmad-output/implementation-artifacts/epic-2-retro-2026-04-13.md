## Epic Review

### Outcome

Epic 2 delivered the planned borrower workflow foundation and operator-facing borrower experience. All five stories were completed:

- `2-1` Establish borrower identity and searchable records
- `2-2` Create borrower intake flow
- `2-3` Search and browse borrowers
- `2-4` View borrower details and lending history
- `2-5` Evaluate borrower eligibility for a new application

The epic established a coherent borrower journey from identity creation through search, detail visibility, and new-application readiness guidance without prematurely implementing the downstream application workflow.

### What Went Well

- Story sequencing was strong and each story extended the previous one cleanly.
- Scope control stayed disciplined, especially around avoiding premature Epic 3 workflow behavior.
- Read-side and write-side seams stayed clean through focused use of borrower query and service objects.
- Review cycles improved UX clarity, accessibility, and edge-case handling without creating churn.
- The resulting borrower workflow feels stable, understandable, and ready to support the next epic.

### Challenges

- No major systemic delivery issues surfaced during Epic 2.
- Minor corrections appeared during review around UX polish, accessibility semantics, deterministic behavior, and precise edge-case messaging.
- These corrections were normal refinement signals rather than blockers or signs of instability.

### Key Insights

- Disciplined vertical slicing worked well: build the smallest trustworthy foundation first, then extend it story by story.
- Review-driven polish remains valuable even in a smooth epic because it catches correctness and UX clarity issues before they accumulate.
- The existing controller/query/service boundaries are supporting steady feature growth and should be preserved in later workflow-heavy epics.
